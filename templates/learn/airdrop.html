Airdrop aboutℳonero

Distributing a cryptocurrency token or coin, usually for free, to numerous wallet addresses. [1] In a typical ICO, participants contribute capital to buy tokens whereas, in an airdrop (or hard fork), tokens are allocated to existing holders of a particular chain—typically Bitcoin or Ethereum. Instead of buying tokens, they’re simply given away to the holders of another coin. [2]
